Definition: Lingua Franca is a term used to describe a language that is adopted as a common means of communication between people who speak different native languages. Historically, the original "Lingua Franca" was a mix of Italian, French, Spanish, Greek, and Arabic used for. Today, English is considered the most widespread modern lingua franca, used in international business, science, travel, and diplomacy.
